# Meeting Script — Kylene Handoff

Short script for Levi's meeting with Kylene to walk through what's live, what she can do, and what comes next.

---

## 1. What's Live

"Your website is live at bowtienetwork.com. Everything we discussed is there — the homepage, vendor pages, testimonials, the contact forms. When someone fills out a contact form, it goes straight to your Google Sheet."

"Your email, domain, and DNS are all untouched. Google Workspace works exactly like before."

## 2. What You Own

"You own the domain, the email, and the lead data. The Google Sheet with leads is yours. We're temporarily hosting the website and database on our company AWS account for the next 2–3 months so you don't have to worry about infrastructure right now."

## 3. The Admin Panel

"We built you a separate admin panel where you can manage your vendors and testimonials yourself. It's at a different URL from your main website — completely hidden from the public."

"I'll send you the login link, your email, and a temporary password. First thing you should do is log in and change your password."

"Once you're in, you can:
- Add or edit vendors — change their name, description, logo URL, website link
- Toggle vendors on or off, mark them as 'coming soon'
- Add or edit testimonials — pick which ones are featured on the homepage
- See a dashboard that shows you content health — like which vendors are missing logos"

## 4. What It Costs

"Right now, nothing. We're covering hosting for the next 2–3 months."

"If you take over hosting later, the estimated cost is about $5–$15 per month for the web hosting, and the database is free. Your domain renewal and Google Workspace stay the same as before. Total would be roughly $5–$17 per month."

"If you ever want to move to a simpler platform like Vercel, that's about $20/month but the setup is easier. We can help with that when the time comes."

## 5. What She Should Do Next

1. "I'll send you the admin login securely. Don't share it over email."
2. "Log in and change your password right away."
3. "Go through your vendors — update any missing logos or descriptions."
4. "Mark your favorite testimonials as 'Featured' so they show on the homepage."
5. "Try submitting a test lead through the contact form and check your Google Sheet."

## 6. What Requires Developer Help

"The admin lets you manage vendors and testimonials. But if you want to change the homepage layout, add new pages, or change the design, that still needs developer work. Same for setting up Google Analytics or adding image upload capability — those are optional future improvements."

## 7. Timeline

"We'll review the hosting arrangement around the end of July. Before then, we'll talk about whether you want to take over the AWS account, move to a different platform, or continue the current setup."
